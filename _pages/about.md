I'm an incoming PhD student in Transportation Engineering at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in 2026 Fall, advised by [Prof. Bin Ran](https://engineering.wisc.edu/directory/profile/bin-ran/). I'm now a Research Assistant in Department of Computer Science at New York University Shanghai, advised by [Prof. Zhaonan Wang](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=fNdU6I0AAAAJ&hl=zh-CN&oi=ao). I received my M.S. in Transportation Engineering from Tongji University, and B.S. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Tongji University.

## Research Interest
My research interests span the following areas:

1. **Traffic Prediction**: Applying advanced AI methods for prediction. My previous studies include real-time crash prediction and electric vehicle charging demand prediction using LSTM-based and GNN-based techniques, respectively.
2. **Causal Inference**: My previous work includes designing a semi-data-driven Bayesian network structure learning algorithm and summarizing the five aspects of model interpretability in traffic crash analytics. I am also very familiar with concepts, assumptions, frameworks and methods of causal inference.
3. **Uncertainty Quantification**: Conformal Mixture of Experts was proposed to measure the model uncertainty for EV charging demand prediction.

More information can be found in [Publications](https://yifanwang1017.github.io/publications)
